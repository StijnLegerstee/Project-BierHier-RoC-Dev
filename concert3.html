Mariah Carey Concert

Mariah Carey focuses on the music during Caution tour
Before 2016, Mariah Carey did not visit the Netherlands for a concert in 13 years,
but ever since she keeps coming back to the Ziggo Dome in Amsterdam.
After 2016’s Sweet Sweet Fantasy tour, Carey returned two years later with her Christmas show.
Thursday night, precisely six months later, Carey ended the European leg of her Caution World Tour here.
Although she sings some some of the material of this record, her best album since 2005‘s The Emancipation of Mimi,
she mostly serves the hits the fans came to hear.

Through the years Carey experienced some backlash over performances with people stating that
she could not sing anymore and the only time of year when her music gets the attention it deserves in the media,
is when ‘All I Want For Christmas Is You’ breaks another streaming or radio record. During her show in the Ziggo Dome however,
she still is a super star. She confirms her reputation before she even walks on the stage, as she arrives fashionably late,
30 minutes later than she was supposed to.

She opens with ‘A No No’, a sassy, yet breezy R&B anthem taken from her latest album Caution.
Although the tour was named after this record, the only other song from it she performs,
is the title track, while ‘Stay Long Love You’ functions as an interlude during which Miss Carey is backstage for a costume change.
Throughout the night she wears four different dresses and a bathing suit like attire.
She is accompanied by a talented group of male dancers and a full band with three backing vocals.
They give Mariah’s hits a soulful and swinging vibe.

Carey herself does not really participate in the choreography, but walks up and down the stage,
concentrating on belting out her biggest hits. Those dizzying high notes and big ad libs might not
come as easy as in the earlier days of her career anymore, but she does still very much stand her ground vocally.
She does hit the iconic whistle notes in ‘Emotions’ and gives vocally satisfying renditions of hits
like ‘Dreamlover’, ‘Always Be My Baby’ and ‘One Sweet Day’,
which she sings with band members Trey Lorenz and Daniel Moore. Big ballad ‘My All’,
a challenging song to sing even for greats like Mariah Carey,
is performed flawlessly and receives some of the loudest applause of the evening.
Carey takes us all the way back to the beginning of her career in 1990,
as she performs her first ever single ‘Vision Of Love’. She impressively works her way through the big notes
and leaves her audience waiting in anticipation, while postponing the big finale.
When she does hit that note, the Lambily (as she calls her fans) cheer her on.
